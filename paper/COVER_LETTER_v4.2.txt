## Motivation and Novelty

The NLRP3 inflammasome is a validated therapeutic target for a broad spectrum
of chronic inflammatory disorders — type-2 diabetes, atherosclerosis,
Alzheimer's disease, gout, non-alcoholic steatohepatitis — yet no FDA-approved
NLRP3-targeting drug is available. The Phase-II clinical failure of MCC950
due to hepatotoxicity (DILI) highlights a critical need for computational
frameworks that co-optimise potency AND drug-likeness during virtual screening.
Our work addresses this need through three principal contributions:

1. **Hierarchical trimodal molecular representation.** We integrate sequence
(Mol2Vec), topology (D-MPNN), and geometry (SphereNet) via a hierarchical
fusion module (cross-modal attention + gated fusion + low-rank bilinear +
learnable importance network) that performs sample-level modality reweighting.
This design overcomes the modality-collapse and lack-of-adaptivity limitations
of static concat/gated fusion.

2. **Joint activity–ADMET multi-task learning.** Beyond the primary activity
task, we simultaneously predict five ADMET drug-likeness indicators (Lipinski
RO5, QED, PAINS, synthetic accessibility, LogP moderation) in a unified model.
Under matched conditions (Table 5.3), multi-task training reduces the 5-seed
ROC-AUC standard deviation by 42% and improves ensemble F1 by +0.020,
MCC by +0.028 and Recall by +0.037 — directly implementing the modern
"activity and safety in parallel" paradigm and yielding actionable DILI
risk predictions for every candidate.

3. **Rigorous dual-protocol evaluation with honest applicability-domain
characterisation.** We report performance under both Bemis–Murcko scaffold
splitting (primary, ROC-AUC 0.9167) and random splitting (reference, ROC-AUC
0.9267 ± 0.0107; 5-seed ensemble 0.9408) on the same curated dataset. All
data-curation criteria — including removal of five published inhibitors and
their Tanimoto ≥ 0.7 neighbours — were fixed a priori, and the test set was
never used for model or data selection. Early-recognition performance is
strong (BEDROC@α = 20 = 0.9028; EF@5% = 3.47 vs. theoretical upper bound
N/P = 3.7612).

## Honest Reporting of Applicability-Domain Limits

A distinctive feature of our submission is an explicitly *transparent*
treatment of the applicability domain. On external validation against the
five published NLRP3 inhibitors held out from training, STG-Mol recovers
2/5 at the default threshold (MCC950, CY-09); the three deeply out-of-domain
scaffolds (Tranilast, OLT1177, Oridonin) receive appropriately low confidence,
with predicted probability trending positively with Tanimoto distance to
training data (Spearman ρ = 0.80, Pearson r = 0.96, n = 5). We frame this
2/5 recall as an applicability-domain limit of any single-target model
trained on the current public NLRP3 corpus — dominated by MCC950-class
diarylsulfonylureas — and propose an AD-gated deployment strategy that
routes in-AD compounds through STG-Mol and out-of-domain chemotypes through
orthogonal similarity/pharmacophore search. We believe this level of
transparency about model limits is critical for the field and directly
addresses concerns raised by the QSAR / cheminformatics community about
over-optimistic reporting.

## Clinical and Practical Impact

Applying STG-Mol under the dual-precision cascaded protocol to 8.8 million
ZINC compounds, we identify eight in-silico NLRP3 candidates supported by a
complete multi-level computational evidence chain: AutoDock Vina docking
(mean ΔG = −8.87 kcal/mol; three candidates below −9.4), 100 ns molecular
dynamics simulations (GROMACS, AMBER99SB-ILDN + GAFF2), MMPBSA free-energy
analysis, and multi-task ADMET assessment. For each candidate, the ADMET
module actively flags DILI risk (predicted ≥ 0.808 for all eight compounds),
providing direction for lead optimisation — a capability directly motivated
by the MCC950 Phase-II failure. Prospective wet-lab validation of these
candidates is planned as our immediate next step.

## Fit to Computers in Biology and Medicine

Our study integrates deep learning (multi-modal / multi-task architecture),
computational chemistry (docking, MD, MMPBSA free-energy), and translational
medicine (NLRP3 pharmacology, DILI risk assessment). This interdisciplinary
scope directly aligns with the journal's mission of publishing rigorous
computational studies with demonstrable clinical relevance. The complete
code, curated dataset, and per-seed prediction artefacts are publicly
available on GitHub (https://github.com/dongleil/STG-Mol), together with a
one-command reproduction workflow, enabling full reproducibility and
community extension.

## Erratum Disclosure (v4.1 → v4.2)

In an earlier internal draft (v4.1) we reported EF@1% = 3.76. On our
random-split test set (N = 252, P = 67) this value does not exceed the
theoretical upper bound N/P ≈ 3.7612, but top-k = 3 molecules is too
narrow an interval for a stable estimator — a single true-positive
displacement shifts EF@1% by ~1.0 in absolute terms. In the current v4.2
manuscript we therefore report EF@5% / EF@10% / EF@20%, with an explicit
erratum note in Table 5.1c documenting this correction. All numbers were
recomputed via a verified script (`scripts/recompute_metrics_v42.py`)
that asserts `EF ≤ N/P` at run time to prevent recurrence.
